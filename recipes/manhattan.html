Manhattan

Description

No one knows for certain who created this cocktail or where. Despite
its mysterious history, it likely came into being sometime around the
1880s as a combination of rye whiskey, sweet vermouth and aromatic
bitters. While many bartenders today substitute bourbon for rye,
thereâ€™s nothing like the original.

Ingredients

  • 2 oz rye whiskey
  • 1 oz sweet vermouth
  • 3 dashes Angostura bitters
  • Garnish: brandied cherry

Steps

  1. Add the rye whiskey, sweet vermouth, bitters, and ice into a mixing glass, then stir
  2. Strain into a chilled Nick & Nora or coupe glass
  3. Garnish with a brandied cherry
